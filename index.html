My Story . . .

I’m Kimie, welcome to my kitchen!

A little about me… I’m a child of YHWH, a wife, a mama to 2 little gals, among other things. I love food (cooking and baking it too), I love to read, I love to worship the Lord with my voice, and I love spending time in the sunshine.

Well, the reason I got into baking (and cooking) in the first place is my Grandma. My Grandma was the queen baker and cook of the family. Whenever we went for a visit, she had all kinds of homemade snacks and treats prepared, cooked us from-scratch meals (her chicken stew… yummm), and can’t forget her stove-popped popcorn with extra butter. And then we start talking about holidays and she really outdid herself - TONS of pies, cookies, cake, snack mix, along with the turkey and/or the ham. I think you get the picture. =)

She became ill towards the end of her life and so I started trying to make her recipes to fill in the gap. She went home to Jesus in 2012 at which point I took it upon myself to carry the torch (or maybe the whisk?), if you will, and be the resident family baker. Those are some pretty big shoes to fill but I’m doing my best. That was when I really started to take it more seriously and the love has grown since. Being in the kitchen brings back memories of her and sometimes I’ll shed a few tears (because I miss her so) but mostly just makes me smile. When I bake for someone, it’s like my love language to them – my way of showing them I care (just as I feel it was for my Grandma).

I’ve often gone back and forth on selling some baked goods but I never wanted to lose the love. I never wanted it to become burdensome. Then, I started baking scones. I’ve made them on occasion over the years but just this year, I thought it seemed like such a niche baked good. Good scones are hard to find. Before I started baking my own, I often thought of them as dry and crumbly.

Oh but not these… these are moist and buttery on the inside, while crisp and flaky on the outside. They are so good and have become a bit of an obsession. I started making different flavors weekly for my family and it’s been something we all look forward to. I feel scones are pretty special and I hope they bring you joy as they have for us!

Thanks for stopping by and Godspeed!

-Kimie
